PARAGLIDING

  1. Bir Billing (Himachal Pradesh)

    2. Bir Billing in Himachal Pradesh is a world-renowned paragliding destination, offering thrilling flights with stunning views of the Dhauladhar Range and Kangra Valley.
    The take-off point is Billing, and the landing site is Bir, with ideal weather conditions for flying from March to June and September to November.
    Tandem paragliding flights last 15-30 minutes and cost between ₹2,500 to ₹5,000 ($30-$60). For those interested in learning, paragliding schools offer training and certification.
    Bir Billing is also known for its adventure sports, including trekking and camping, making it a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.

  2. Kamshet (Maharashtra)

    3. Kamshet, located in Maharashtra, is a popular yet relatively quieter paragliding destination, around 110 km from Mumbai.
    Known for its ideal flying conditions, it offers stunning views of rolling hills, lakes, and villages. Paragliding here is suitable for both beginners and experienced pilots.
    The best time to visit is from October to May. The cost of tandem paragliding in Kamshet ranges from ₹2,500 to ₹5,000 ($30-$60) for a 15-30 minute flight, depending on the operator and season.
    Training courses for beginners typically range from ₹10,000 to ₹20,000 for a few days of instruction.

  3. Nandi Hills (Karnataka)

    4. Nandi Hills, located near Bangalore in Karnataka, offers a serene and thrilling paragliding experience.
    Known for its gentle winds and stunning views of the surrounding hills, valleys, and forests, it is an ideal spot for both beginners and experienced pilots.
    The take-off point is located at the hilltop, providing panoramic views of the countryside. The best time to visit for paragliding is from October to March.
    Tandem paragliding flights in Nandi Hills typically cost around ₹2,500 to ₹4,000 ($30-$50) for a 15-20 minute flight, with training courses available for those looking to learn the sport.

  4. Pondicherry (Tamilnadu)

    5. Pondicherry offers a unique paragliding experience, with the opportunity to fly over beautiful beaches and the scenic coastline.
    The gentle sea breezes and stunning views of the Bay of Bengal make it an ideal destination for adventure seekers.
    Paragliding in Pondicherry typically takes place at Paradise Beach or Serenity Beach, offering both tandem flights for beginners and training options for those looking to learn.
    The best time to visit for paragliding is from October to March. Tandem flights usually cost between ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 ($35-$60) for a 15-20 minute flight, depending on the operator.

BUNGEE JUMPING

  1. Jodhpur (Rajasthan)

    2. Jodhpur, Rajasthan, offers a unique bungee jumping experience at Mehrangarh Fort. The jump takes place from a height of 150 feet, with breathtaking views of the historic fort, the blue city,
    and the surrounding Aravalli Hills. This combination of adventure and scenic beauty makes it a standout spot for thrill-seekers.
    The cost of bungee jumping in Jodhpur typically ranges from ₹2,500 to ₹4,000 ($30-$50) for a single jump, depending on the operator.
    It’s a safe and professionally managed experience, making it a memorable and exhilarating activity for visitors to the city.

  2. Goa

    3. Goa offers an exciting bungee jumping experience at Anjuna Beach, one of its most popular destinations.
    The jump is from a 25-meter-high platform, providing a thrilling free-fall with the beautiful beach and Arabian Sea as a backdrop.
    This location offers a unique blend of adventure and coastal scenery, making it a popular choice for both locals and tourists.
    The cost of bungee jumping in Goa typically ranges from ₹3,000 to ₹4,500 ($35-$55) for a single jump, depending on the operator and season.
    It's a safe and professionally guided experience, perfect for those seeking an adrenaline rush in a scenic setting.

  3. Lonavala (Maharashtra)

    4. Lonavala, Maharashtra, offers a thrilling bungee jumping experience at Della Adventure Park, one of the highest bungee jumping spots in India.
    The jump takes place from a 135-foot-high platform, surrounded by the lush greenery of the Western Ghats, offering a stunning view of the landscape.
    This adventure park also features other activities like zorbing and dirt biking, making it a popular destination for adrenaline seekers.
    The cost of bungee jumping in Lonavala is typically ₹3,000 to ₹4,000 ($35-$50) for a single jump.
    It's a safe and exhilarating experience, perfect for both first-timers and seasoned jumpers.

  4. Coorg (Karnataka)

    5. Coorg, Karnataka, offers an exciting bungee jumping experience at the Coorg Adventure Park, located in the scenic coffee estates.
    The jump takes place from a 100-foot-high tower, offering a thrilling free-fall with views of the lush green hills and surrounding forests.
    Known for its natural beauty, Coorg provides a serene yet adventurous setting for those seeking an adrenaline rush.
    The cost of bungee jumping in Coorg generally ranges from ₹1,500 to ₹2,500 ($18-$30) for a single jump, depending on the operator.
    It's a safe, guided activity, making it a great option for adventure enthusiasts visiting this picturesque region.

RIVER RAFTING

  1. Rishikesh (Uttarakhand)

    2. Rishikesh, Uttarakhand, is India’s most popular destination for white-water rafting on the Ganges River.
    The experience includes thrilling rapids ranging from Grade II to IV, with scenic views of the Himalayan foothills.
    The most popular rafting route is from Shivpuri to Rishikesh, offering an exciting mix of calm waters and challenging rapids.
    The best time to visit is between September to June. The cost for rafting in Rishikesh typically ranges from ₹300 to ₹1,500 ($4-$20) for shorter routes,
    and up to ₹3,000 to ₹4,500 ($35-$55) for longer, more challenging stretches, depending on the operator.

  2. Teesta River (Sikkim)

    3. The Teesta River in Sikkim offers an exhilarating rafting experience through the beautiful landscapes of the eastern Himalayas.
    The river provides a mix of calm and challenging rapids, making it ideal for both beginners and experienced rafters.
    The rafting stretch from Melli to Teesta Bazar is especially popular, offering Grade II to IV rapids.
    Rafting on the Teesta River allows adventurers to enjoy lush greenery, valleys, and the scenic beauty of the region.
    The cost of rafting on the Teesta typically ranges from ₹1,500 to ₹3,000 ($20-$40), depending on the stretch and operator.
    The best time for rafting is from March to June and September to November.

  3. Tungabhadra River (Karnataka)

    4. The Tungabhadra River flows through Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh, known for its scenic beauty and cultural significance.
    It is famous for the Tungabhadra Dam and the ancient ruins of Hampi along its banks, a UNESCO World Heritage site.
    The river offers a thrilling rafting experience with moderate rapids. Rafting prices generally range from ₹600 to ₹1,200 per person for a 1-2 hour ride,
    depending on the operator. Full-day adventure packages, including rafting and other activities, can cost between ₹2,500 to ₹4,500 per person.
    The best time for rafting is from October to March.

  4. Alaknanda River (Uttarakhand)

    5. The Alaknanda River is one of the primary tributaries of the Ganges, flowing through the scenic hills of Uttarakhand.
    Known for its majestic beauty and challenging rapids, it offers a thrilling rafting experience.
    The river passes through deep gorges and forests, making it a popular spot for both adventure and nature enthusiasts.
    Rafting prices on the Alaknanda generally range from ₹1,000 to ₹2,500 per person for a short trip (2-3 hours).
    For more extensive packages, including camping and other activities, prices can go up to ₹4,000 to ₹6,000 per person.
    The best time for rafting is from March to June and September to November.

SCUBA DIVING

  1. Diu

    2. Diu, located on the western coast of India, offers unique scuba diving experiences with clear waters and rich marine life.
    Known for its coral reefs, shipwrecks, and vibrant underwater ecosystems, Diu is an ideal destination for both beginners and experienced divers.
    Popular dive sites include Nagoa Beach and Pani Kotha, where divers can encounter various species of fish, corals, and sometimes, sea turtles.
    Scuba diving prices in Diu typically range from ₹2,500 to ₹4,000 for a single dive, with packages including training and equipment rental.
    The best time to visit is from October to May.

  2. Cochin (Kerala)

    3. Cochin, located in Kerala, offers a unique scuba diving experience in its calm and clear coastal waters.
    The dive sites around Munambam and Kumarakom are known for their vibrant coral reefs, diverse marine life, and occasional shipwrecks,
    making it a great destination for both beginners and seasoned divers. Scuba diving in Cochin allows you to explore tropical fish, sea turtles,
    and rich underwater biodiversity. Prices for scuba diving in Cochin typically range from ₹2,500 to ₹4,000 for a single dive, depending on the operator.
    Packages with training and equipment rental can cost around ₹5,000 to ₹7,000. The best time to visit is October to May.

  3. Lakshadweep Islands

    4. Lakshadweep, an archipelago in the Arabian Sea, offers some of the most pristine and unspoiled scuba diving experiences in India.
    Known for its crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and abundant marine life, it’s an ideal destination for divers.
    Popular dive sites include Agatti, Bangaram, and Kavaratti, where you can encounter species like sea turtles, manta rays, and colorful reef fish.
    Scuba diving prices in Lakshadweep range from ₹3,000 to ₹6,000 per dive, depending on the island and dive operator.
    Packages that include multiple dives and equipment rental can cost between ₹10,000 to ₹20,000. The best time to visit is from October to April.

  4. Havelock Island (Andaman & Nicobar Islands)

    5. Havelock Island, part of the Andaman & Nicobar Islands, is renowned for its crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and diverse marine life,
    making it a top scuba diving destination in India. Popular dive sites like Ritchie's Arch, Kalapathar Beach, and Elephant Beach offer opportunities to see exotic marine species,
    including barracudas, turtles, and Scuba diving prices in Havelock typically range from ₹3,000 to ₹6,000 per dive,
    with options for introductory courses for beginners. Package deals, including multiple dives and equipment, may cost between ₹8,000 to ₹15,000.
    The best time to visit is from October to May.
